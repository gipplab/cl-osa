Iran to complain about U.S.-radio at the Hague tribunal

Iran will protest to the International Court of Justice at [[the Hague]] and other global bodies about the U.S.-funded [[Radio Free Europe]], the Iran Daily reported Monday.

It quoted Foreign Minister [[Kamal Kharazi|Kamal Kharrazi]] as saying the radio ``was set up to interfere in Iran's internal affairs.''

It did not say when the complaints will be filed. The English-language daily also did not say which organizations Iran will approach besides the [[International Court of Justice|ICJ]] in [[Netherlands|the Netherlands]].

On Tuesday, Iran recalled its [[Ambassador|ambassador|0.2]] from [[Prague]] to protest the [[Czech Republic|Czech|0.6]] government's assistance to the radio and said it would reduce the level of its [[Economy|economic|0.2]] and [[Politics|political|0.2]] cooperation with Prague.

The American-sponsored station also began broadcasts directed to [[Iraq]] on Friday. In a trial period of several weeks, the station will broadcast one 30-minute program a day to Iran and Iraq.

The [[Persian language|Farsi|0.6]]-language service to Iran was approved by the Czech government in August.

Radio Free Europe began transmitting from [[Munich|Munich, Germany]], in 1951, spreading un[[Censorship|censored|0.2]] news to [[Soviet Union|Soviet|0.2]]-controlled countries behind the [[Iron Curtain]] during the [[Cold War]] between the [[Western world|West|0.2]] and the [[Communism|Communist]] [[Eastern world|East|0.2]].
