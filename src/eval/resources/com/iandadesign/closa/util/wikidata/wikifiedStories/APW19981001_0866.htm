Hungarian government and Jewish communities agree on compensation

The [[Hungary|Hungarian]] government and the Federation of [[History of the Jews in Hungary|Hungarian Jewish]] Communities signed an agreement Thursday on collective compensation for [[Communism|communist|0.6]]-era confiscations, the state news agency [[Magyar TÃ¡virati Iroda|MTI|0.4]] reported.

The financial basis for the funding is real estate that belonged to the Jewish communities and fell victim to [[Nationalization|nationalization|0.4]] under the communists.

Instead of getting back the actual property _ 152 schools and other buildings _ the assessed value of which is 13,511,000,000 [[Hungarian forint|forints]] (dlrs 63 million), the Jewish community collectively is to get a percentage annually, starting with the current year, when 608 million forints (dlrs 2.9 million) will be paid.

``The money will be mainly used for education, religious services, [[Social welfare provision|social welfare|0.2]], health service, and charity ends,'' Gusztav Zoltai, executive director of the Federation, said.

There are between 80,000 and 100,000 Jews in Hungary. Some 600,000 were killed in [[the Holocaust]].

When the communists assumed full power in 1948, they started a wave of nationalizations that spared no one, including the Jewish community.

On June 20, 1997, a similar agreement, the first of its kind, was signed between the Hungarian government and the [[Holy See|Vatican|0.6]].
