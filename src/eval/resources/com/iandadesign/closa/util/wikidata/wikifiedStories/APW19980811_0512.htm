Police identify substance in [[Tea|tea|0.4]] poisoning case

The poison that sickened 10 Japanese company employees after they drank tea brewed from the same water pot was a [[Toxicity|toxic|0.2]] [[Sodium|sodium compound|0.2]] that affects the [[central nervous system]], [[Law enforcement in Japan|police|0.2]] said Tuesday.

Police still had no clues to who might have put the chemical in the pot, which had been filled with water between Friday night and Monday, they added.

The 10 workers at Xyence Co., a [[Lumber|wood preservative|0.2]] company in [[Niigata Prefecture|Niigata]], 256 kilometers (159 miles) northwest of [[Tokyo]], were hospitalized Monday after suffering [[nausea]] and [[Paresthesia|numbness|0.2]] during their morning tea break.

The case gave Japan another shock two weeks after [[Curry|curry|0.2]] laced with [[cyanide]] and [[arsenic]] killed four people at a festival in southwestern Japan. It also raised fears of [[Copycat crimes|copycat|0.4]] attacks.

Lab tests on the tea revealed traces of [[sodium azide]], a chemical used in many industrial products, including [[Airbag|air-bag|0.2]] mechanisms, said police spokesman Masashi Toma.

The victims of the poisoning were in stable condition, but three suffered mild [[Hepato-biliary diseases|liver disorders|0.2]] Tuesday, said Yasuo Hirose, an official at the [[Niigata, Niigata|Niigata City]] Hospital.

The [[1995|March 1995|0.2]] [[Nerve agent|nerve gas|0.4]] attack on Tokyo subways carried out by the [[Aum Shinrikyo|Aum Shinri Kyo]] religious cult has made Japan particularly wary of poisoning incidents. The subway gassing killed 12 people. (js)
