Airport ready for operation; Palestinians await word from Israel

The new [[Palestinian people|Palestinian]] [[Airport|airport|0.2]], a powerful symbol of fledgling independence, is ready to open, the head of the Palestinian [[Civil aviation|Civil Aviation|0.2]] Authority said Friday after an operations drill with [[Israel|Israeli]] officials.

[[Yasser Arafat International Airport]] in the southern [[Gaza Strip]] was to have opened last week as part of the [[Israeli-Palestinian conflict|Israeli-Palestinian peace agreement|0.2]]. However, Israel fell behind schedule when it held up Cabinet ratification of the accord by two weeks.

Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu has said the airport would start operating next week, but has not set a date.

On Thursday, Israeli and Palestinian officials staged a four-hour drill, said Fayez Zeidan, head of the Palestinian [[Civil Aviation Authority|Civil Aviation Authority|0.2]].

About 100 Palestinian airport employees and 50 [[Israelis]] who will share [[Airport security|security|0.2]] duties played the roles of passengers and airport personnel. For lack of a plane, the ``passengers'' arrived by [[Bus|bus|0.2]].

Zeidan said the exercise covered arrival and departure procedures, including passport checks as well as customs and luggage inspection.

He said the airport was ready for operation. ``We just need to hear from the Israelis,'' Zeidan said. sa-kl
