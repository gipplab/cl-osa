PORT DICKSON, Tues. - [[Federal Government|Federal|0.2]] and [[State|State|0.2]] [[Government agency|Government agencies|0.2]], given the task to undertake [[Economy|economic|0.2]] projects under the Development Programme for the Hardcore Poor (PPRT), must ensure their successful [[Implementation|implementation|0.2]].

[[Rural|Rural|0.2]] Development Minister Datuk Annuar Musa said yesterday those responsible should closely monitor the implementation of the projects and ensure that the [[Resource allocation|allocation|0.2]] provided by the [[Ministry (government department)|ministry|0.2]] benefit the hardcore [[Poverty|poor|0.2]] participants.

He said those responsible, including district officers, must personally check on the progress of the projects being carried out.

``We don't want to see projects as being a success on paper but on inspection, they don't show the desired results,'' he added.

According to him, it was the ministry's policy now that the [[Minister (government)|minister|0.2]], deputy minister, parliamentary secretary, director-general and other senior ministry officials had to visit the PPRT projects to see what was happening to the allocation given by the Government.

Annuar said this after visiting a [[Mussel|mussel|0.4]] rearing project in Kuala [[Lukut]] today as part of his day-long visit to several PPRT projects in [[Negeri Sembilan|Negri Sembilan]].

Present were Chembong Assemblyman Firdaus Harun and [[Port Dickson]] district officer Hassan Nawawi Abdul Rahman.

Annuar added that the implementing agencies should also conduct in-depth [[Research|studies|0.2]] of the projects proposed to ensure their viability.
