Nikhil Chakravarty, a top Indian political columnist, dead at 74

Nikhil Chakravarty, one of [[India]]'s best known [[Journalist|journalist|0.2]]s, has died. He was 74.

Chakravarty was suffering from [[Brain tumor|brain cancer|0.8]] and died in hospital on Saturday, his family said.

[[Politics|Politics|0.4]] was his forte and his columns appeared in leading [[List of newspapers in India|Indian newspapers|0.2]] and magazines.

Chakravarty also started a weekly news magazine, ``Mainstream,'' in 1962 and edited it for more than three decades.

Last year, the government appointed him chief of [[Prasar Bharati]], an autonomous [[Corporation|corporation|0.2]] set up to end government control of radio and television.

Chakravarty declined a top government award in 1990, saying no journalist should be identified with the governing establishment.

He graduated from [[University of Calcutta|Calcutta University]] and studied at [[Merton College, Oxford|Oxford University's Merton College|0.8]] in England.

Chakravarty taught history at Calcutta University in the 1930s before taking to [[Journalism|journalism|0.2]] as correspondent of a weekly brought out by the [[Communist Party of India]].

He later joined the CPI and remained a member until 1978.

He closed the Mainstream weekly for a while when Prime Minister [[Indira Gandhi]] imposed emergency rule in India 1975-77, jailing political opponents and imposing censorship on newspapers and magazines.

Chakravarty was born November 3, 1913, in India's northeastern state of Assam.
